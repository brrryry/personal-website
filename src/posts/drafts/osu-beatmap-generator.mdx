## The Idea & Original Thinking
This idea was formulated and briefly worked on about a year and a half ago. I recruited one of my friends, and we developed a basic idea.

The maps that users download are made by other people, and they are downloaded as an <a href="https://osu.ppy.sh/wiki/en/Client/File_formats/osz_%28file_format%29">.osz</a> file. 
However, we found out through some research (two google searches) that .osz files are just ZIP files. Extracting from an .osz file reveals the contents of each beatmap
in the form of an <a href="https://osu.ppy.sh/wiki/en/Client/File_formats/osu_%28file_format%29">.osu</a> file, along with any other assets (images, audio files, etc.) used in the beatmap. \
\
From here, we can take the beatmap's audio file and turn it into structured data - a spectrogram. Our original spectrogram was a constant-Q spectrogram, and we didn't think about the specific
type of spectrogram that much. We went with default settings - 84 bins, spanning 7 octaves of notes (logarithmic scaling of frequencies to adjust to human perception). \
\
