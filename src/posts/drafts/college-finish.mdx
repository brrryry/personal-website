It really doesn't feel like it's been that long, but the time has come for me to graduate from college (at least for now).
Now that I'm free for a little bit, I wanted to take a moment to reflect and jot down some of my thoughts and feelings throughout my tenure.
Forgive me if this feels more like a diary entry than a blog post, but I think that's what it is. Most of my thoughts here
will be less filtered and more personal. \
\
(I will still remove names for the sake of privacy.)

<br />
## 0.1 - Acknowledgements
The most prominent feeling that I have is gratitude. There are so many things that I am grateful for,
and I want to ramble a bit on that. \
\
**I am grateful to my family** for emotionally and financially supporting me throughout my college years.
I'm extremely lucky, and I constantly remind myself of how privileged I am to have 
such a support system. Whether it was relaxing with me while I finished my homework assignments or
navigating social situations, they were always there for me. My success now - and therefore most of my future successes - are
largely owed to them. \
\
**I am grateful to my friends** - both present and past - for being 
there for me. It would probably take me hours to list all of the people to which I owe my gratitude.
Some friends were there to help me feel more comfortable, while others forced me to step out of my comfort zone.
Some friends were there to have stupid and funny conversations, while others were there to teach me something introspective. \
\
Most friends were some combination of all of these things. \
\
Regardless, I am grateful for every moment that I got to spend, and while it saddens me that I will inevitably
lose contact with some of these people, I can only hope that it was worth it for them too. \
\
**I am grateful to my professors** for teaching me. They educated me - not just in 
the subjects that I took, but also on how to grow as a person with a prospective career.
I didn't just learn about computer science - I learned about the world of research,
academia and the industry. The pond is much bigger than I thought, and it would not have been
possible for me to stabilize myself in it without their help. \
\
Thank you.

<br />

## 0.2 - Regrets
Behind the feeling of gratitude, there is also unfortunately a feeling
of longing and regret. There are so many things that I could have done differently. \
\
If I had planned some things better, I would have finished my Bachelor's degree with a mathematics minor. 
I missed it by one class. \
\
If I had gone to Rutgers or NJIT, I could have spent a lot less money.
On the other hand, if I had taken my Transfer Pathway Program offer at Georgia Tech,
I could have had a more prestigious degree for the same price. 
Don't get me wrong, Stevens is great. However, I can't help but wonder of the possibilities. \
\
If I had taken a thesis with my Masters, I could have had a better shot
at getting into a PhD program. Looking back, I definitely had the time, but I didn't take advantage of the opportunity. \
\
This definitely hurts a bit, but to be honest, the social regrets hit a bit harder. \
\
If I had been better at time management, I could have had
so many more memories to cherish. I could have gone to more events
and hung out with more people. \
\
If I was more attentive to messages, I could have saved
friendships that I lost contact with. There are 
some people that I no longer talk to, and honestly, that's on me. \
\
There may be a bit more on these subjects as I go on with this post, but that's enough for now.
Moving on...time to dissect the past and talk about what that means for the present.

## 1 - Exploration
My first year at college was my time to go join as many clubs as possible and explore what I really wanted to do.
As a CS major with 14 credits (where the average was 17-18), I had a lot of free time compared to my peers. \
\
The first friend that I made was my roommate. He was pursuing a different major, but we got along well. While I unexpectedly found myself enjoying the presence of another person,
I also got the luxury of having a separate bedroom. We were both somewhat introverted and laid back, and we had similar interests. Looking back, I was so lucky to have found someone that
compatible as a random roommate. \
\
During our orientation, the two of us went around and made other miscellaneous friends. We got to know our dorm neighbors and some other random people
on campus that we happened to meet. I am still in contact with some of these people (even if it's not on a regular basis), and I enjoy hearing
regular updates from them. 
<br />

The first club that I joined was the **Stevens Badminton Club**. I had played racket sports before, so I had a solid foundation
to build on. The people I met there were - and still are - some of my closest friends. Because I was relatively friendly and outgoing to the 
general body, I got to be involved in the club's leadership through regular hangouts outside of the club.
